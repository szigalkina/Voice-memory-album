# Voice Baby Album

## Register
Hybrid. The journal (recording, editing, settings) is **product**: it must disappear
into a one-thumb task done by a tired parent. The album book (and signin/onboarding)
is **brand**: the design is part of the keepsake itself.

## Users
Two parents sharing one private album of their baby's first year. Mobile-only in
practice. Often one-handed, often at night, often mid-feed.

## Purpose
Talking is the whole interface: a parent records a voice note, AI turns it into a
book page (title, message, quote, milestone), photos attach to the moment. The
album must feel worth printing and keeping for decades.

## Brand personality (target, set 2026-07-19)
Quiet luxury, editorial, heirloom. Reference: **Artipoppe** — ecru and ink,
fashion-serif typography, photography-first, sparse copy, no visual noise. The
product should feel like an object you would gift, not an app.
Retained from the earlier direction: the tactile ritual of tap-to-record and one
quiet moment of delight when a milestone is detected. Expressed with restraint
(no emoji, no confetti).

## Anti-references
- Baby-pastel app-store aesthetic (mint/pink, rounded bubbles, emoji chrome)
- SaaS dashboard grammar (cards everywhere, colored badges, gradient accents)
- Scrapbook kitsch (stickers, washi-tape borders, marker handwriting)

## Strategic principles
1. The baby's content is the only color. Interface stays bone + ink.
2. One brand mark: the engraved voice-waveform hairline. No other ornament.
3. Typography does the luxury: large light serif, micro-tracked caps for labels.
4. Every screen must still work one-handed in the dark, in 5 seconds.
